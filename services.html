Most organisations do not have an AI technology problem. They have an operating-model problem: workflows, decision rights, governance and cost discipline that were designed for a world before AI. We help you redesign how your organisation works, using your own AI use cases as the proving ground.

Start with a workshop

Every engagement begins with a workshop of one to three days, depending on where your organisation is starting from. We bring your leaders, or your engineers, up to a shared understanding of what AI changes about how work gets done: how teams should be shaped, how quality is assured, how cost is owned, and where the real decisions sit. You leave with a common vocabulary, a clear-eyed view of your starting position, and a prioritised list of where to begin. Workshops are priced as a fixed fee.

Build a use-case pipeline worth funding

Most organisations already have a list of AI use cases. The problem is rarely the list; it is how the list is ranked and resourced. AI changes the economics of planning itself: capacity is no longer measured in headcount alone, budgets must account for running costs that scale with usage rather than seats, and the cheapest use case to build is often not the most valuable one to learn from. We help you establish a use-case pipeline as a permanent feature of the operating model, with a prioritisation discipline that weighs business value against what each case will teach you about how the organisation must change. Identifying, ranking and funding use cases stops being an annual planning exercise and becomes a continuous capability.

Prove it with a small number of teams

We then run a coaching and implementation programme, typically three to six months, starting with two or three teams and the use cases at the top of that pipeline. Each use case is built as a working capability, but it is also a deliberate test of your operating model: we use it to expose how decisions, roles, governance and costs need to change. Our coaches work embedded alongside your people throughout, on both sides of the problem: the business side (prioritisation, governance, measurement, change) and the technical side (architecture, engineering practice, evaluation, cost control).

The programme works across the areas where AI puts the operating model under most strain. Policy: redesigning internal governance so that approval, risk and assurance keep pace with how AI work is actually done, rather than queuing behind processes built for a slower world. Cost: treating the running cost of AI, paid for in tokens rather than servers and seats, as an engineering property with a named owner, measured and optimised from the first build. Culture: the leadership behaviours, incentives and frontline adoption that determine whether new ways of working take hold or quietly revert. And capability: coaching your engineers and architects into the new shape of their roles, and codifying what is built into evaluated, reusable skills your teams keep.

What you keep is not a deck. It is a proven operating model: a new way of prioritising, budgeting and organising that your own teams have tested on real work, and that enables company-changing speed and responsiveness. The capabilities you build along the way are real and stay in production. But the durable asset is the way of working itself.

It is the operating model, not any single use case, that compounds.

Scale across the organisation

Once the operating model is proven with the first teams, we help you roll it out: more teams, more use cases drawn from the pipeline, the same patterns. Because the approach is codified from the start, each wave is faster and cheaper than the last, and your dependence on us falls as your own people take over the running.

How we charge

Workshops carry a fixed fee. The coaching and implementation programme is also fixed-fee, sized by two things: how many teams you want to scale to, and how deeply you want the new operating model embedded. For larger programmes, a shared-outcome model is available: a capped percentage of validated, measured savings, such as reductions in AI running costs or cycle time, earned only when the results are real and measured. Either way, you have a known ceiling.

Our incentives sit exactly where yours do.
